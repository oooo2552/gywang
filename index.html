Guan-Yun Wang is an assistant professor at Fu Jen Catholic University. She received her Ph.D. degree from Tohoku University in 2023. She was a member of the WISE program (Doctoral Program for World-leading Innovative & Smart Education by MEXT) for AI electronics at Tohoku University. She received her B.Comm., B.A., and M.S. degrees from National Taiwan University in 2018 and 2020, respectively. She has work experience at Sumika Technology Ltd. Co. as a senior engineer handling digital transformation projects in the factory. Her research interests include education technology, AI application, human-computer/robot interaction, digital transformation, computational linguistics, and computer vision. .

Interests

My research interests lie in how people change their behavior with the aid of technology. In my Ph.D. study, I focused on students' learning using custom-developed educational webpages. I estimated students' learning states, including their engagement levels and help-seeking behaviors, through computer vision techniques.

I also have industry experience, having worked as a senior engineer in a digital transformation department. We implemented new technology and AI tools to improve production processes and office work within the company, achieving significant efficiency gains.

My research objective and general goals are to determine how technology improves human life and behavior, fostering positive outcomes, motivation, and processes. I developed technology tools, such as robots and web applications, to conduct experiments and implement them in real-world settings.

Currently, I am interested in large language models and their applications. To fulfill the requirements of AI agents, I am considering implementing this technology in various human contexts. I am enthusiastic about new technologies and their applications, especially their impacts on human behavior.

Welcome to collaborate with me to develop new AI tools/applications or explore new research avenues.
